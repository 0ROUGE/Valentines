A Letter To You

My love,

There is a quiet strength in what we share. It reminds me of the way Fitzgerald once wrote, “I love her and that’s the beginning and end of everything.”

What I feel for you is not loud or fleeting. It is steady. It is chosen. It grows in the in-between moments — in conversations that stretch past midnight, in shared silence that feels full rather than empty.

Jane Austen once said, “You must allow me to tell you how ardently I admire and love you.” If I could borrow those words, I would. Because loving you feels less like chaos, and more like coming home.

With you, even ordinary days carry meaning.

Will you be my Valentine?
